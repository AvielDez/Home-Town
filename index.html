Tucson

Region

Sonoran Desert

When you think about a desert, you might think of a hot, dry, dusty place. That’s not the Sonoran Desert. It has tree-covered mountains, grassy plains, and hot valleys. It even has rivers, but many of them don’t have water all the time.

Sonoran Weather

The Sonoran region is a hot desert! If you are willing to stick around you can witness the spectacular monsoon storms scattered throughout the Tucson area. Be beneath the storm or appreciate them from a distance with plenty of high areas for the best views.

Sonoran Natural Life

Famous for the Saguaro Cactus, Tucson and the surrounding area is full of unique plant and animal life. From scarce desert valleys filled with mammals and dangerous reptiles to canyons and mountains filled tall forrest trees, mountain lions and bears.

Things To See

The Sonoran Desert Museum

Zoo, botanical garden, art gallery & classes, natural history museum, and aquarium. This Museum is a must see! Even the drive to get there just after a monsoon rain is a wonderful experience all on its own.

Mount
Lemmon

Visitors to this biodiverse Sky Island are delighted by the hiking, skiing and rock climbing — just an hour from Tucson, Arizona. Mount Lemmon offers an escape from the city for metropolitan Tucsonans.

San Xavier
Church

Mission San Xavier del Bac is a historic Spanish Catholic mission located about 10 miles south of downtown Tucson. The church was founded long before Arizona became a state. Over 200 years old! The thick walls provide a great escape from the heat.

Great Food

Prep & Pastry

Great place to start your day. They have the best breakfast/brunch food completely local to the Tucson area with great service, atmosphere and good drink selections. I would personally recommend the 'Cast Iron Duck Confit'

Mi Nidito

A tradition in Tucson's Mexican restaurants, Mi Nidito features authentic sonoran cooking. You may have a short wait during peak dining hours, but it's well worth the wait. Service at Mi Nidito is as warm as a desert summer day.

Maynards Downtown
Tucson

If you want to get fancy then this is the place. Nestled in the historic train depot, Maynards offers an authentic dining experience featuring locally-inspired dishes, an award-winning wine list, and hand-crafted cocktails.

Activities

Cycling

The Tucson Loop, extends for over 100 miles and connects to the beautiful Oro Valley and the Catalina Mountains. These natural cycling sites provide another 80 miles of biking routes and well-kept streets with cycling lanes.

Hiking

Is Tucson good for hiking? YES! With five mountain ranges surrounding town and a national park, Tucson is a great place to put your boots on. I cannot recommend this enough. If you enjoy hiking, you'll love Tucson.

Awe at City Lights

This was one of my favorite pass times. At night, find a main road in Tucson called Campbell Ave. Follow this road towards the northern mountains until you hit a loop. You'll be forced to turn around and be pleasantly surprised by the city lights.
